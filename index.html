Three Things You Were Direct About
⏱️
A Price Increase is a Six-Week Project
One minute per customer to generate a price sheet. Fifty thousand customers. Days of production system time — because JDE simulates a sales order for every single customer-item combination to produce a price sheet.
"If you want a June 1 price increase, you have to start your work in February — just to get through the system work."
📉
Every Day You Wait, You're Eating Margin
Global raw materials. Tariffs. Closed shipping lanes. Oil prices. Some years three or four price increases needed in a single product line. The market moves faster than JDE can respond.
"Every day that you don't increase your price, you're eating into your margins. Speed to market becomes paramount."
📈
The Problem Gets Worse With Every Acquisition
Fifteen acquisitions in five years. More items, more customers, more locations, more discount programs — all loading into a pricing engine that was already customized in a way that makes it harder to scale, not easier.
"It only gets worse as we continue to grow as a company."
🤔
You Also Said: "I've Never Seen This Done. I'm Skeptical It Can Be."
You were honest that you've never seen pricing successfully pulled out of a core ERP — that integrating pricing and order management outside of JDE is something you're skeptical anyone can actually pull off. Why This Architecture — IPS's Three Options

IPS identified three paths forward. Here is how they compare.

Architecture Rationale
Option 1 — Fix It Inside J.D. Edwards

Optimize the existing pricing module inside J.D. Edwards — fewer customizations, faster processing, better maintainability.

The challenge:

The complexity is already deeply embedded. More items, more customers, more acquisitions — the engine that is already stretched gets stretched further. This path buys time, but doesn't change the underlying architecture.

Option 2 — Custom Integration to External Pricing

Build a custom integration between J.D. Edwards and a third-party pricing engine — bespoke middleware, hand-coded and maintained by IPS's I.T. team.

The challenge:

Trading one set of complexity for another. Every acquisition adds to a custom integration that has to be rebuilt each time the business grows.

Option 3 — Salesforce OMS + PriceFx ✓ Recommended

A purpose-built external pricing engine connected to a certified order management layer — both with pre-built J.D. Edwards connectors, maintained by the vendors, not IPS's I.T. team.

Why this works:

J.D. Edwards stays as the system of record. The integration is certified, not custom. Every new acquisition plugs into the same architecture — no rebuild required.

  What This Architecture Specifically Solves for IPS
⚡
Speed to Price
A pricing update that previously required weeks of J.D. Edwards system time becomes a rule change in PriceFx — live in Salesforce Order Management immediately. IPS's commercial team owns it. No I.T. ticket required.
🏢
Acquisition-Ready by Design
Fifteen acquisitions in five years means the architecture has to absorb new brands without a new implementation project each time. With this model, a new brand's pricing is a rule set — not a J.D. Edwards configuration engagement.
🤖
The Agentforce Path
Once pricing constructs are in place, Agentforce can automate what today requires a team — pricing updates across product lines, intelligent order routing, anomaly detection before problems escalate. This is the architecture that makes that possible.
💡
On Zilliant and Conga
Both came up in IPS's evaluation. Zilliant is a capable pricing tool, but those implementations tend to be large, bespoke, and I.T.-intensive — trading one set of custom complexity for another. Conga is built around CPQ and configure-price-quote workflows, which is not the problem IPS is trying to solve. This is a pricing engine and order management play, not a CPQ play.

What PriceFx + Salesforce Order Management Delivers for IPS

The core advantages of this architecture — grounded in what IPS said it needs.

Why This Wins
⚡  Pricing Speed IPS Needs

PriceFx gives IPS's commercial team direct ownership of every pricing rule — contracts, volume tiers, customer programs, discount structures. A pricing update that today takes weeks inside J.D. Edwards becomes a change your team makes in minutes, live immediately in order management. Every day of speed to market is margin recovered.

J.D. Edwards remains the system of record for inventory, fulfillment, and financials. Salesforce Order Management integrates with it — it doesn't replace it. The inventory checks, order commitment logic, and back-order handling all stay in J.D. Edwards. The integration handles the handoff cleanly.

Fifteen acquisitions in five years means the architecture has to absorb new brands without a new implementation each time. PriceFx handles multi-brand, multi-entity pricing natively. A new brand's pricing is a rule set configuration — not a J.D. Edwards project. Salesforce Order Management routes orders to the right J.D. Edwards entity automatically.
