Three Things You Were Direct About
⏱️
A Price Increase is a Six-Week Project
One minute per customer to generate a price sheet. Fifty thousand customers. Days of production system time — because JDE simulates a sales order for every single customer-item combination to produce a price sheet.
"If you want a June 1 price increase, you have to start your work in February — just to get through the system work."
📉
Every Day You Wait, You're Eating Margin
Global raw materials. Tariffs. Closed shipping lanes. Oil prices. Some years three or four price increases needed in a single product line. The market moves faster than JDE can respond.
"Every day that you don't increase your price, you're eating into your margins. Speed to market becomes paramount."
📈
The Problem Gets Worse With Every Acquisition
Fifteen acquisitions in five years. More items, more customers, more locations, more discount programs — all loading into a pricing engine that was already customized in a way that makes it harder to scale, not easier.
"It only gets worse as we continue to grow as a company."
🤔

You were honest that you've never seen pricing successfully pulled out of a core ERP — that integrating pricing and order management outside of JDE is something you're skeptical anyone can actually pull off. Why Salesforce Order Management — Not NetSuite, SAP, or a JDE Extension

OMS is the irreplaceable center of this architecture. The pricing engine only works as well as the order layer it feeds into.

Salesforce OMS — Competitive Analysis
⚙️
PriceFx makes the price. Salesforce OMS makes the order happen.
Getting the right price is only half the problem. The order still has to be validated, split across IPS's brands, routed to the right JDE entity, tracked end-to-end, and surfaced to the distributor in real time. That orchestration layer — the one that sits between your distributors and JDE — is exactly what Salesforce OMS was built for. Without it, a pricing engine has nowhere to send the order.
  What Salesforce OMS Does That No Other Option Matches for IPS
🔀
Multi-Brand Order Splitting
When Ferguson orders Weld-On cement and Studor valves in a single cart, OMS automatically splits the order into separate JDE entities (WO-US and ST-US) with the correct routing logic per brand — and presents it to Ferguson as one unified order with one tracking number. No other platform handles this without custom code.
🔗
Native PriceFx + JDE Bridge
Salesforce OMS is the only order layer with certified integrations to both PriceFx and JDE simultaneously. It calls PriceFx for price validation at the moment of order submission, then passes a clean, price-confirmed record to JDE — no manual reconciliation, no middleware build, no batch sync delay.
📡
Distributor Visibility Layer
Once an order enters JDE, it's a black box. OMS creates a live visibility layer on top — distributors see real-time status, split shipment tracking, return management, and invoice history through the portal. This is the experience your distributors benchmark you against. JDE alone cannot surface this.
