The Challenge
Most marketing AI was built
for the wrong business model
The catalog wall
AI personalization tools are architected for SKU-based e-commerce. Businesses built on custom, made-to-order products are being forced to retrofit a model that was never built for them.
The lean team squeeze
Marketing teams managing multiple brands with the same headcount as single-brand teams spend the majority of their time on tasks AI should be handling.
Retention is still time-based, not behavior-based
Most retention programs trigger on days-since-purchase — not on what the customer actually ordered, what that predicts about their next need, or when they're most likely to reorder.
We hear this consistently across our customer base — and we've seen what breaks the pattern.
The Reframe
Your product complexity isn't a liability.
It's your richest behavioral dataset.
Every unique spec combination a customer builds — material, size, finish, quantity, turnaround — is a high-signal behavioral fingerprint. SKU businesses have catalog data. You have intent data. The companies winning at retention aren't the ones with the best product catalog. They're the ones who learned to read order patterns as behavioral signals.
SKU-based model Behavioral signal model
Recommends based on what others bought Triggers based on what this customer ordered
Segments by product category Segments by purchase intent fingerprint
Triggered by days-since-purchase Triggered by lifecycle signals and spec patterns
