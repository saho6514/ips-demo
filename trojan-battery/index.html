📍  The Situation

Trojan Battery is a 100-year-old manufacturer serving 8 distinct markets — golf, aerial work platforms, material handling, floor cleaning, RV, marine, transportation, and solar. They are already a happy Salesforce customer, with Revenue Cloud and Commerce Cloud running their commercial operations. But their marketing runs on HubSpot — completely disconnected from the rich customer data already living in Salesforce. The result: every campaign decision is made without knowing what customers actually bought, browsed, or are about to need. And with no dedicated marketing leader currently in place, no one is protecting the status quo.

🔍  What We Know
  • No CMO or VP of Marketing — CCO Laurie Oswald owns marketing by title, but her entire career is in enterprise sales (Vertiv, CommScope). She thinks in pipeline and revenue.
  • PE-backed (C&D Technologies / KPS Capital Partners) — financial discipline and ROI scrutiny are built into every technology decision.
  • Centennial year in 2025 — leadership is publicly talking about transformation and the next 100 years. Oswald appeared on multiple podcasts on "leadership & transformation."
  • 8 markets, 8 buyer personas, 8 distributor networks — all receiving the same undifferentiated HubSpot communications.
⚡  The Opening
  • No marketing executive to defend HubSpot — the internal blocker doesn't exist right now.
  • Revenue Cloud + Commerce Cloud already in place — this is a "complete the platform" conversation, not a rip-and-replace.
  • Marketing Cloud Next shares Data Cloud with their existing products — the integration is native, not a project.
  • The pitch is a revenue and efficiency story — frame everything in pipeline contribution and distributor activation, not brand or demand-gen.
⚠️  The HubSpot Data Risk — Share This With Keith
"On August 4, 2026, HubSpot is expanding its data discovery and intelligence features to bring more robust and reliable data to our customers. To power these tools, enrichment data such as business contact details, employer information, and email deliverability signals, may be shared with other customers."

HubSpot quietly opted all customers in to cross-customer data sharing effective July 1st. Trojan Battery's distributor contacts, dealer relationships, and channel partner data — built over 100 years — may now be enriching HubSpot's broader network. This isn't a scare tactic. It's something a trusted advisor makes sure their customer knows about, especially during a marketing leadership transition when no one is actively watching the account settings.

No additional headcount. That's the efficiency story Laurie Oswald will respond to.

3

Consolidate on a platform that proves ROI

PE-backed companies consolidate technology spend. Trojan already trusts Salesforce with their commercial operations. Adding Marketing Cloud Next isn't adding a vendor — it's removing one (HubSpot) and getting a platform where marketing, sales, and commerce data finally speak the same language. The ROI case writes itself.
